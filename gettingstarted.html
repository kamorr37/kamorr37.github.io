Time to Start Fermenting!

Gather your supplies and follow the instructions below to get started on your sourdough journey.
Remember, patience is key, and soon you'll be enjoying your own homemade sourdough bread!

Supplies Options Amount Notes
Flour All-purpose, Bread, Rye or a combination 80 grams total or about 1/2 cup I use half rye and half bread flour. Rye helps slow down the fermentation process, allowing for more time between feedings.
Water Room Temperature 80 grams total or about 1/2 cup If you don't have a kitchen thermometer, ensure the water is not cold and is lukewarm.
Mixing Bowl Any size or shape. 1 Don't be picky, as long as it's clean you can even use a coffee mug!
Large Glass Jar Any jar, no matching lid required. 1 The smaller your jar, the more likely it is that your starter could overflow as it becomes more active.
Jar Covering Plastic wrap or a breatheable lid 1 If using plastic wrap, poke a few holes in it to let air in. If using a lid be sure it is not on tightly, so fermentation gases can escape!
Kitchen Scale Measuring cups 1 or a set While most bakers prefer to measure in weight for accuracy, using measuring cups is totally fine! Sourdough is hearty!
Rubber Spatula Any sturdy spoon to mix with 1 Dough will be sticky! Choose something that can be easily cleaned.

Initial Instructions:

  1. In your mixing bowl, mix the flour and water until no dry flour remains.
  2. Transfer the mixture to your large glass jar.
  3. Cover the jar with plastic wrap or a breathable lid, ensuring it is not sealed tightly.
  4. Let the jar sit at room temperature (ideally around 70-75°F or 21-24°C) for 24 hours.
  5. After 24 hours, check for bubbles. If you see bubbles, it's a good sign that fermentation has started. If not, give it another 12-24 hours.
  6. Once you see bubbles, it's time to feed your starter.

Feeding & Maintenance Instructions:

  1. Add about 30 grams, or a tablespoon and 1/2 of the bubbly started to your mixing bowl and add 80 grams of flour and 80 grams of water. Mix well and discard the leftover starter.
  2. Cover the jar again and let it sit at room temperature for another 24 hours.
  3. Repeat the feeding process every 24 hours for about 5-7 days, or until your starter is bubbly, has doubled in size, and has a pleasant, tangy aroma.
  4. Once your starter is active and bubbly, you can switch to feeding it every 12 hours if you want to speed up the fermentation process.
  5. Now you can use it in your sourdough recipes or store it in the refrigerator for longer-term storage.

Congratulations! You've successfully created your sourdough starter. Enjoy the process and the delicious bread that will come from it!
